    Understanding Personalization Types:

    CRITICAL: NHS numbers and merge fields can be acceptable for AdMail IF the content is generic and uniform.
    The key question: Is the MESSAGE CONTENT the same for everyone in the cohort, or unique to this individual?

    Surface Personalization ONLY (CAN BE ADVERTISING):
    * Name and address in header/salutation (e.g., "Dear Mrs. Smith", "Dear ((fullName))")
    * NHS number for delivery/identification (not linked to individual medical data in the letter)
    * Merge fields ((firstName)), ((nhsNumber)) for addressing only
    * Cohort membership reference: "Our records show you haven't attended [screening]" (cohort = non-attenders)
    * Generic health campaign content (same message for all in cohort)
    * Assessment: Surface personalization + uniform generic content + clear CTA → ADVERTISING

    Content Personalization (BUSINESS MAIL):
    * Individual-specific health conditions: "You have diabetes", "Your asthma requires..."
    * Individual test results: "Your HbA1c result is 7.2%", "Your cholesterol is..."
    * Individual appointment details: "Your appointment is on ((appointmentDate)) at 2pm"
    * Individual medical advice unique to this person
    * Merge fields for individual medical data: ((appointmentDate)), ((testResult)), ((medication)), ((diagnosis))
    * References beyond cohort membership: "Your GP record shows you have diabetes AND hypertension"
    * Assessment: ANY individual-specific medical content → BUSINESS

    KEY DISTINCTIONS:

    "Our records show...":
    - "Our records show you haven't attended cervical screening" → Cohort membership (non-attenders) → CAN BE ADVERTISING
    - "Our records show you have diabetes" → Individual condition → BUSINESS
    - "Our records show your last HbA1c was 7.2%" → Individual result → BUSINESS

    "You are due for..." vs "All [cohort] are eligible for...":
    - "You are due for your Shingles vaccination" → Individual eligibility determination → BUSINESS
    - "All residents aged 70-79 are eligible for Shingles vaccination" → Cohort eligibility → CAN BE ADVERTISING

    Confidentiality:
    - "[Confidential - For Patient Only]" → Individual-specific communication → BUSINESS
    - No confidentiality marker + generic message → CAN BE ADVERTISING

    <royal_mail_guidance>
    Royal Mail Guidance: Core Eligibility Criteria for Admail.

    A letter qualifies as Advertising Mail (Admail) if all the following conditions are met:
    * The content is largely the same for all recipients (uniform message).
    * The primary purpose is promotional/informational: promoting the sale or use of products or services, or encouraging support or donations to a cause.
    * The message is unsolicited or not paid for by the recipient (i.e. not part of a subscription or membership delivery).
    * It is primarily informational (e.g. bills, statements, policy notices).
    * It is part of a public service duty (e.g. tax reminders, council notices).
    * Non-personalised Surveys intended to improve a product or service.

    Disqualifying Factors (Makes it BUSINESS MAIL):
    * Individual-specific medical content: test results, diagnoses, treatment plans unique to this person
    * Individual appointment confirmations with specific dates/times/locations
    * Transactional content (fulfilling orders, confirmations)
    * Individual medical records beyond cohort membership: "You have diabetes", "Your HbA1c is 7.2%"

    NOT Disqualifying (Acceptable for AdMail):
    * NHS numbers for delivery/identification (if content is generic)
    * Merge fields (()) for name, address, NHS number only (if content is generic)
    * Cohort membership: "You haven't attended [screening]" (cohort = non-attenders, content is same for all non-attenders)
    * Generic health campaigns to cohorts: non-attenders, 65+, geographical groups

    Examples of Eligible Mailings (ADVERTISING):
    * Catalogues or brochures sent unsolicited to promote seasonal offers
    * Unsolicited newsletters or magazines not tied to a subscription or membership
    * Loyalty scheme mailings offering discounts or reward redemption
    * Discount vouchers or event invitations sent to encourage purchase or attendance
    * Unsolicited product samples aimed at generating future sales
    * NHS cohort-based health campaigns (even with NHS numbers and merge fields for delivery):
      - "Dear ((fullName)), NHS: ((nhsNumber)) - All cervical screening non-attenders are invited to order free HPV self-test kit. Scan QR code!"
      - "Dear Mrs. Smith, NHS: 123456789 - All 65+ residents eligible for free flu vaccine. Book at [URL]"
      - "Dear ((firstName)), You haven't attended bowel cancer screening. Order free test kit now!"
    * General health promotion campaigns with strong CTA, even if targeted to cohorts (non-attenders, age groups, geographical areas)

    Examples of Ineligible Mailings (BUSINESS):
    * Bills, statements, or order confirmations
    * Subscription or member-only publications
    * Fulfillment items such as loyalty cards, tickets, or invitations already purchased
    * Census or data profiling surveys
    * Shareholder reports or AGM notices
    * Individual appointment confirmations with specific dates/times:
      - "Your appointment is on ((appointmentDate)) at 2pm with Dr. Smith"
    * Individual test results:
      - "Your HbA1c result is 7.2%, which indicates..."
    * Individual medical advice:
      - "Based on your diabetes diagnosis, you need to..."
    * Letters referencing individual medical conditions beyond cohort membership:
      - "You have asthma and diabetes. You need flu vaccine because of your conditions."

    Additional Rules of Thumb:
    * FOCUS ON CONTENT, NOT IDENTIFIERS: NHS numbers and merge fields are acceptable if content is uniform
    * Surface personalization (name/address/NHS number) does not disqualify if content is uniform and generic
    * Cohort membership ("you haven't attended screening") is NOT individual medical content
    * Strong CTA (QR code, booking link, phone) strengthens AdMail eligibility
    * If the message CONTENT differs for each person (individual conditions, results, appointments), it's BUSINESS
    * If the message CONTENT is uniform (same screening invitation for all non-attenders), it's ADVERTISING
    </royal_mail_guidance>
